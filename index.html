03 — The Structural Vulnerability

Five Overlapping Pain Points.

India's fraud crisis is not a data problem. It's a pre-payment verification problem. These five structural gaps exist across every payer simultaneously.

🔗
Broken Clinical Loop
Physician notes ⇄ nursing charts ⇄ vitals ⇄ investigations ⇄ treatment ⇄ pharmacy — the loop is never closed in real time. Bills get paid against incomplete or unverified clinical artefacts.
⏱️
"Pay First, Audit Later" Model
Claims are paid before any clinical-billing cross-check. Phantom services, upcoding, and duplicate claims pass undetected. Recovery after payment: less than ₹20 per ₹100 lost.
📋
Multiple Payers, Inconsistent Rules
PM-JAY packages, CGHS rate lists, ECHS polyclinic workflows, ESIC tariff schedules — billing teams juggle 6+ rule sets. "Coding to the highest payer" is inevitable without a unified pre-payment engine.
📄
Illegible Unstructured Documents
Handwritten discharge summaries, faded photocopies, missing consultant stamps — OCR-unfriendly and un-auditable at scale. Document forensics cannot catch what cannot be read.
👻
Phantom & Fabricated Claims
Services never delivered; fake ECHS admission cards; forged pathology reports (Gujarat case); 29 identical radiology reports across a single admission. Each approved without clinical verification.
Hard Evidence — Oct 2025 to Apr 2026

04 — Detection Architecture

Six Engines. One Firewall.

All six stages run before the payment authorisation gate. Traditional audit firms recover <20% months later — FraudIQ stops 90–95% of fraudulent payouts before they happen.

STAGE 01 · TEAL
Ingestion & Triage
Every incoming claim is OCR-parsed, fingerprinted, de-duplicated, and assigned a FIQ risk score before any human reviews it.
Document hash + line-item fingerprint catches re-submitted claims under new IDs
Completeness gate: missing consents, blank severity scores, absent clinical fields
Blended risk score: provider history × claim-vs-peer ratio × document completeness
🇮🇳 Catches: PM-JAY claims resubmitted under new patient IDs after denial
STAGE 02 · BLUE
Member & Benefit Vault
Claim matched to the beneficiary's coverage, full claims history, prior authorisations, and EHR — including cross-payer coordination of benefits.
Eligibility, plan validity, sub-limits and exclusions vs diagnosis + place of service
Pre-auth vs final claim diff — LOS creep and scope expansion flagged
Cross-payer COB: detects same episode billed to CGHS and private TPA simultaneously
🇮🇳 Catches: ECHS fake-card identity reuse; CGHS + TPA dual filing
STAGE 03 · PURPLE
Document Forensics
Before trusting any clinical data, FraudIQ authenticates every document — empanelment IDs, consultant signatures, scan metadata, physiologically impossible values.
Signature & credentials graph — NPI / CGHS ID vs procedure billed vs facility registration
Impossible-value detector: vitals that cannot physiologically coexist
Identity contradictions: blood-group mismatches, ECG machine header vs patient file
🇮🇳 Catches: fabricated MRI reports; fake ECHS cards; forged NABL accreditation logos
STAGE 04 · RED · CORE
Clinical Cross-Match
The detection core. Bill reconciled against every clinical artefact — physician notes, nursing charts, vitals, labs, drug charts, operative notes — scored for medical necessity.
Bill vs notes vs labs vs drugs vs vitals — every mismatch scored and flagged
Medical necessity scoring against PM-JAY HBP packages / CGHS clinical standards
Lab-vs-drug safety: e.g. Meropenem 1g q8h at eGFR ~12 → 6× overdose + billing anomaly
🇮🇳 Real audit: drug dose 6× excess + phantom billing caught simultaneously

Every code and price is audited against India-specific rules — PM-JAY package ceilings, CGHS rate lists, ECHS/ESIC formularies, unbundling and NCCI-equivalent edits.
